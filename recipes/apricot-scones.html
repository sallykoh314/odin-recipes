Apricot Scones

Description

I have never been a big fan of scones because I often found them too dry. The thought of the dry scone getting caught in my troat steered me away from making scones. What makes this scone different than others is the moistness of the pastry without taking away the crumbly characterists of a scone. Not only that but the subtle flavors of the star anise and tumeric make this scone very pleasant and enjoyable.

This recipes creates about 12 scones and they can be kept in the freezer portioned out. To enjoy fresh baked scones, all you need to do is take them from the freezer and place them on the baking sheet and into the oven without thawing them out. To top it off, make a simple glaze with powdered sugar and a little apricot soaking liquid and glaze the scone as soon as they come out of the oven.

Ingredients

  • 1000g All purpose flour
  • 100g Sugar
  • 9g Baking Powder
  • 6g Baking Soda
  • 6g Salt
  • 2g Turmeric
  • 2g Ground star anise

  • 450g Butter, cold and cubed

  • 481g Heavy cream

  • 438g Apricots, soaked and diced

Method of Production

  1. Soak the apricots over-night in hot water with one star anise and 1 table spoon of vanilla paste.
  2. Strain the apricots and reserve the soaking liquid for the scone glaze.
  3. Preheat the oven to 325 degrees Fahrenheit.
  4. Mix all the dry ingredients in a mixing bowl with a paddle attatchment.
  5. Add the cold cubed butter into the dry ingredients and mix until the flour and butter resembles wet sand.
  6. Add the heavy cream and mix until the dough forms but still crumbly. It is important not to overmix the dough.
  7. Add the apricots and mix until just incorporate. Again, do not overmix.
  8. Let the dough sit in the fridge for an hour and scoop with a icecream scooper.
  9. Place the scooped scone dough onto a parchment lined baking sheet and make sure to leave space betwee the scones.
  10. Brush the top of the scones with some heavy cream and bake for about 20 - 25 minutes.
  11. Take them out of the oven and glaze the top of the scones letting the glaze run down to the sides and enjoy!
